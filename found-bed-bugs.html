What to Do When You Find Bed Bugs ‚Äî First 24 Hours Action Plan

Published on September 26, 2025 | 12 min read | CleanSure Experts
Finding bed bugs is stressful, but your response in the first 24 hours determines how quickly and effectively the problem gets resolved. Follow this step-by-step action plan to contain the situation and get professional help.

üö® First Things First: Don't Panic

Discovering bed bugs feels overwhelming, but remember: this is a solvable problem. Thousands of families deal with bed bugs every year and return to normal life. Your quick, calm response will make treatment more effective and less disruptive.

The actions you take in the first 24 hours after discovering bed bugs significantly impact the success of treatment and prevent the problem from spreading to other areas of your home. This guide walks you through exactly what to do, when to do it, and what mistakes to avoid.

Immediate Response: The First Hour

1
Stop and Document (5 minutes)

Take photos of what you found. Use your phone to capture clear images of bugs, stains, or evidence. These photos help professionals assess the situation and can be useful for landlord communication or insurance claims.

2
Contain the Area (10 minutes)

Resist the urge to start moving things or throwing items away. This can spread bed bugs to clean areas. Close off the affected room if possible and avoid moving items out of it.

3
Assess the Scope (15 minutes)

Carefully inspect other sleeping areas, focusing on mattresses, furniture seams, and areas near beds. Don't tear apart your whole house ‚Äî just check the most likely spots.

4
Contact Professionals (10 minutes)

Call a licensed pest control professional immediately. Many companies offer same-day inspections for bed bug emergencies. The sooner you get expert help, the easier treatment becomes.

What NOT to Do ‚Äî Critical Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå DON'T Do These Things

  • Sleep in a different room
  • Throw away furniture immediately
  • Use bug bombs or foggers
  • Spray DIY pesticides
  • Move items to other rooms
  • Wash everything at once
  • Tell everyone you know right away

‚úÖ DO These Things Instead

  • Stay calm and document evidence
  • Contact professionals immediately
  • Contain the affected area
  • Continue sleeping in your own bed
  • Wait for professional assessment
  • Research treatment options
  • Prepare for professional inspection

Why These "DON'Ts" Matter

‚ö†Ô∏è Sleeping Elsewhere Backfires

If you sleep in a different room, bed bugs will follow you. They're attracted to carbon dioxide and warmth from sleeping humans. Moving to the couch or guest room just spreads the problem to new areas.

Other common mistakes that make problems worse:

  • Throwing away furniture: Often unnecessary and can spread bugs to hallways or other areas
  • Bug bombs/sprays: These push bed bugs deeper into wall voids and spread them to new areas
  • Moving items: The quickest way to turn a bedroom problem into a whole-house problem
  • Washing everything: Without proper heat treatment, you may just be moving bugs around

Within the First 24 Hours

Immediate Containment Strategy

üõ°Ô∏è Containment Protocol

Your goal is to prevent bed bugs from spreading while you wait for professional help:

  • Seal the room: Close doors and minimize traffic in and out
  • Strip bedding carefully: Place directly into sealed plastic bags
  • Vacuum visible bugs: Use a small hand vacuum if available, then seal the contents
  • Remove unnecessary items: Only if they can be heat-treated immediately
  • Mark infested areas: Use tape or markers to help professionals focus their inspection

Safe Laundry Protocol

If you must wash items immediately, follow this heat-treatment protocol:

  1. Transport safely: Use sealed plastic bags to carry items to laundry
  2. Sort by heat tolerance: Separate items that can handle high heat
  3. Wash in hot water (120¬∞F+): When fabric allows
  4. Dry on high heat for 40+ minutes: This kills bed bugs at all life stages
  5. Store in clean bags: Keep treated items separate until the area is professionally treated

üí° Heat is Your Friend

Bed bugs die at temperatures above 118¬∞F. Your dryer's high heat setting (140¬∞F+) is more effective than washing alone. When in doubt, dry first, then wash if needed.

Preparing for Professional Inspection

Information to Gather

Professionals work more effectively when you provide detailed information:

üìã Pre-Inspection Checklist

  • Where you found evidence: Specific locations, furniture, or rooms
  • When you first noticed signs: Timeline of discovery
  • Recent travel history: Hotels, visiting others, or guests in your home
  • Bite patterns: Photos of bites if you have them
  • Used furniture acquisitions: Anything brought in recently
  • Previous pest control treatments: Any DIY attempts or professional services

Questions to Ask Professionals

When you contact pest control companies, ask these important questions:

  • How soon can you perform an inspection?
  • What is your success rate with single treatments?
  • Do you offer chemical-free treatment options?
  • What preparation is required from homeowners?
  • Do you provide a warranty or guarantee?
  • What happens if the initial treatment doesn't eliminate all bed bugs?
  • Are your technicians licensed and insured?
  • Can you provide recent customer references?

Special Situations

If You're Renting

üè† Rental Property Protocol

  • Document everything: Photos, dates, written communication
  • Notify your landlord immediately: Most states require prompt notification
  • Know your rights: Research local laws about bed bug responsibilities
  • Don't admit fault: Let professionals determine the source
  • Request building-wide inspection: Bed bugs often affect multiple units

If You Have Health Concerns

For families with allergies, asthma, or chemical sensitivities:

  • Emphasize the need for chemical-free treatment options
  • Ask about heat treatment or other non-toxic methods
  • Discuss any medical conditions with the treatment provider
  • Consider consulting with your doctor about bite reactions
  • Request advance notice of any chemicals being used

If You Have Pets

Pet safety considerations during treatment:

  • Inform professionals about pets during initial contact
  • Ask about pet-safe treatment options
  • Plan temporary pet relocation if needed
  • Remove pet bedding and toys for heat treatment
  • Discuss re-entry times after treatment

Days 2-7: Follow-Up Actions

After Professional Inspection

Once professionals have assessed your situation, you'll need to prepare for treatment:

üìù Treatment Preparation

  • Follow preparation instructions exactly: Each treatment method has specific requirements
  • Schedule time off if needed: Some treatments require you to leave during application
  • Arrange pet care: If pets need to be removed during treatment
  • Clear access areas: Move items away from walls and furniture
  • Wash and dry indicated items: Following the heat protocol mentioned earlier

Managing Stress and Sleep

Bed bugs are stressful, but you still need rest. Here's how to cope while waiting for treatment:

  • Continue sleeping in your own bed: Moving spreads the problem
  • Use light-colored sheets: Makes it easier to spot new activity
  • Consider temporary protection: Bed bug interceptors under bed legs
  • Practice stress management: This situation is temporary and solvable
  • Limit internet research: Too much reading can increase anxiety

üõèÔ∏è Sleep Strategy

It sounds counterintuitive, but continue sleeping in your regular bed. Bed bugs are attracted to their food source (you), so sleeping elsewhere just teaches them to expand their territory.

Communication Guidelines

Who to Tell and When

Strategic communication prevents panic while ensuring necessary people are informed:

‚úÖ Tell These People Immediately

  • Landlord (if renting)
  • Pest control professionals
  • Your partner/household members
  • Recent houseguests (if relevant)

‚ùå Wait Before Telling

  • Extended family and friends
  • Neighbors (unless in apartments)
  • Employers or school officials
  • Social media connections

Wait until you have professional confirmation and a treatment plan before broader communication. This prevents unnecessary panic and misinformation.

Workplace Considerations

Bed bugs rarely spread through workplaces, but if you're concerned:

  • Inspect work clothes before wearing
  • Consider changing clothes before leaving home
  • Keep work bags in hard-surface areas at home
  • Heat-treat work clothing with regular laundry
  • Only inform HR if you find evidence at work

Red Flags: When to Get a Second Opinion

Most pest control professionals are reputable, but watch for these warning signs:

üö© Red Flags in Pest Control

  • Pressure for immediate payment: Reputable companies provide estimates first
  • Door-to-door solicitation: Especially if they claim to be "in the neighborhood"
  • No inspection before quoting: Legitimate companies assess before pricing
  • Prices significantly below market rate: Quality treatment requires proper equipment and training
  • No licensing or insurance proof: Always verify credentials
  • Guarantees that sound too good: Be wary of "permanent" guarantees without follow-up

Apartment and Condo Specific Actions

Multi-unit buildings require additional considerations:

Immediate Steps

  • Notify building management: They need to assess neighboring units
  • Request building-wide inspection: Bed bugs often affect multiple units
  • Document all communication: Keep records of notifications and responses
  • Research local laws: Many cities have specific bed bug ordinances for rentals

Neighbor Considerations

Handle neighbor communication carefully:

  • Let building management handle notifications when possible
  • Be factual and non-alarmist if you speak directly to neighbors
  • Focus on professional treatment rather than blame
  • Coordinate treatment timing with adjacent units

Financial Planning

Bed bug treatment is an investment in your home and peace of mind:

Understanding Costs

  • Get multiple quotes: Prices vary significantly between companies and methods
  • Factor in preparation time: Some treatments require extensive prep work
  • Consider total cost: Include potential follow-up treatments
  • Ask about financing: Some companies offer payment plans
  • Check insurance: Rarely covered, but worth checking your policy

üí∞ Cost vs. Value

While professional treatment requires upfront investment, it's nearly always more cost-effective than months of DIY attempts, replacing furniture, or dealing with a spreading infestation.

Long-Term Perspective

Remember that this situation is temporary:

"Most families return to completely normal life within 2-4 weeks of professional treatment. The key is getting expert help quickly and following their guidance completely."

Recovery Timeline

  • Week 1: Discovery, professional assessment, and treatment
  • Week 2-3: Monitoring for treatment effectiveness
  • Week 4+: Return to normal routines with ongoing vigilance

Prevention After Treatment

Once your bed bug problem is resolved, implement these practices to prevent reintroduction:

  • Inspect hotel rooms carefully when traveling
  • Heat-treat all clothing after travel
  • Be cautious with used furniture
  • Perform monthly inspections of sleeping areas
  • Maintain regular cleaning routines
  • Educate family members about prevention
