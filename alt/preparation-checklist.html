Treatment Preparation Checklist

Simple steps to prepare your home for bed bug heat treatment. Follow this checklist for the best results.

Good news: Heat treatment requires significantly less preparation than chemical treatments. You do not need to bag all your clothing, empty every drawer, or disassemble furniture. Our heat reaches every corner of your home, eliminating bed bugs at all life stages. Just follow the simple steps below to ensure a safe and effective treatment.

⚠

Items to REMOVE Before Treatment

These items are sensitive to high heat (temperatures reach 130-150 degrees F) and should be removed from the treatment area beforehand.

  • Candles and wax items Including decorative candles, wax melts, and crayons. They will melt at treatment temperatures.
  • Crayons and wax-based art supplies Check children's rooms for crayons, oil pastels, and similar items.
  • Medications and vitamins All prescription medications, over-the-counter drugs, and supplements. Heat can degrade effectiveness.
  • Live plants All indoor houseplants should be moved outside or to an untreated area.
  • Pets (and pet supplies) All pets must be removed, including fish tanks and aquariums. Arrange care for pets during the 6-8 hour treatment.
  • Aerosol cans and pressurized containers Hairspray, spray paint, compressed air, deodorant sprays, and any pressurized cans. Risk of rupture at high temperatures.
  • Ammunition and explosives All ammunition, firearms with loaded chambers, fireworks, and any explosive materials must be removed.
✓

Items that STAY During Treatment

One of the biggest advantages of heat treatment: most of your belongings stay right where they are. No need to bag everything up.

  • All furniture Beds, dressers, couches, chairs, tables, nightstands -- everything stays in place. Our heat penetrates all furniture.
  • Clothing in drawers and closets Leave clothing where it is. The heat will reach items inside drawers and closets. No need to bag or launder everything.
  • Electronics TVs, computers, gaming consoles, and other electronics can remain. Our controlled heat stays within safe ranges for electronics.
  • Books and papers Books, magazines, documents, and paper items are safe at treatment temperatures.
  • Dishes and kitchen items Plates, glasses, pots, pans, and utensils can stay in cabinets. Non-perishable food in sealed containers is fine.
📅

Day-of Treatment Instructions

On the day of your scheduled treatment, follow these steps for a smooth and effective process.

  • Leave your home for 6-8 hours All people and pets must vacate the home during treatment. Our technicians will contact you when it is safe to return.
  • Ensure HVAC system is accessible Our team may need access to your thermostat and HVAC system. Make sure the area around your furnace/air handler is clear.
  • Unlock all rooms and closets Every room in the treatment area must be accessible. Unlock interior doors, closets, and any locked storage areas.
  • Open all drawers and closet doors slightly This helps heat circulate more effectively. Crack drawers and closet doors open a few inches.
  • Remove perishable food Take fresh fruits, vegetables, refrigerated items, and chocolate with you. Sealed canned goods and dry goods are fine.
  • Provide parking access for our equipment truck We need a parking spot reasonably close to an exterior door for our heating equipment and hoses.
